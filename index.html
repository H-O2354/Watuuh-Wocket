Runge-Kutta 4 (RK4) Mathematical Engine

1. Thrust System (Bernoulli & Newton)

$$ F_T = \dot{m}_w \cdot v_e = 2 A_e (P(t) - P_{atm}) $$

$$ v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2(P(t) - P_{atm})}{\rho_{liquid}}} $$

2. Gas Phase (Choked Flow Venturi)

Critical pressure ratio for sonic limit ($\gamma \approx 1.34$ for wet air):

$$ \alpha_c = \left( \frac{2}{\gamma + 1} \right)^{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1}} \approx 0.455 $$

If $P_{atm} / P(t) < 0.455$, flow is choked at Mach 1 ($c = \sqrt{\gamma R T}$).

3. Aerodynamics & Drag

$$ F_D = \frac{1}{2} \rho_{air} v^2 C_D A_{ref} $$

4. Thermodynamics (Polytropic Expansion)

The system undergoes adiabatic expansion where heat transfer is minimal but latent heat of condensation alters $\gamma$.

$$ P(t) \cdot V_{air}(t)^\gamma = \text{const} \implies \frac{dP}{dt} = -\gamma \frac{P(t)}{V_{air}(t)} \frac{dV_{air}}{dt} $$

5. Ordinary Differential Equations of Motion (ODE)

$$ \frac{dx}{dt} = v_x, \quad \frac{dy}{dt} = v_y $$

$$ \frac{dv_x}{dt} = \frac{F_T \cos\theta - F_D \frac{v_x}{v}}{m(t)} $$

$$ \frac{dv_y}{dt} = \frac{F_T \sin\theta - F_D \frac{v_y}{v} - m(t)g}{m(t)} $$

$$ \frac{dm}{dt} = -\rho_{liquid} A_e v_e \quad \text{(Mass depletion rate)} $$

6. Tsiolkovsky Ideal Vacuum Range

Because pressure drops as water is expelled, we use the average exhaust velocity $\bar{v}_e$ derived from adiabatic expansion work $W$:

$$ W = \frac{P_i V_i}{\gamma - 1} \left[ 1 - \left(\frac{V_i}{V_f}\right)^{\gamma - 1} \right] - P_{atm}(V_f - V_i) \implies \bar{v}_e = \sqrt{\frac{2W}{m_{liquid}}} $$

$$ v_0 = \bar{v}_e \ln\left(1 + \frac{m_{liquid}}{m_{dry}}\right) \implies R_{max} = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g} $$

* Chemical mode applies a 0.4x penalty to $\bar{v}_e$ due to foam (two-phase flow) choking.
